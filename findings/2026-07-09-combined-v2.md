## Key Findings

### 1. T2 improved by 7pp: −21% vs old −14%

The soft aging sort key (`hit_ratio + 0.3·log(1+wait_s)`) gives cold T1
requests a continuous wait-time boost rather than an en-masse aging cliff.
This reduces the degree to which T2 requests are displaced by warmer T3/T4
requests, cutting T2 TTFT from 108ms to 98ms.

### 2. T3 improved by 4pp: −45% vs old −41%

Hysteresis prevents the chunk controller from reacting to transient decode
queue spikes, maintaining a more stable prefill budget. Combined with the
soft aging benefit, T3 improves consistently across both trials (79ms, 85ms).

### 3. T4 unchanged: −47% vs −47%

T4 benefit is saturated — both old and new combined achieve the same result.
The fundamental gain comes from chunking freeing T4 requests from being
blocked behind cold prefills; the sort key refinements do not change this.

### 4. T1 slightly worse: −2% vs −7%

The old combined result was a single trial that happened to be favorable at
T1. The comb2 average across two trials is −2%, consistent with the reorder
mechanism's negligible T1 effect seen in all other conditions. The old −7%
was likely noise.

## Conclusion

The updated combined condition (soft aging + chunking hysteresis) is the new
canonical headline result:

| Turn | Improvement vs baseline |
|------|-------------------------|
| T1 | −2% (noise level) |
| T2 | **−21%** |
| T3 | **−45%** |
| T4 | **−47%** |

The T2 improvement over the old combined (+7pp) is attributable to soft aging.
The T3 improvement (+4pp) is primarily attributable to chunk hysteresis.
T4 is saturated in both conditions.
